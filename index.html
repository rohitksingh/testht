Introduction to Electric Propulsion

What would you think if you were told that today’s space travel relies on non-combustible fuel? You may be tempted to think this is science fiction, however this is exactly how the Hall Effect Thruster functions. Once a spacecraft is in space, this invention applies about the same amount of force that you would feel holding a stack of 10 quarters or a single tennis ball. (10   16   18) Over time in the frictionless void of space, the spacecraft is slowly and continuously accelerated to extreme velocities, up to 200,000 mph. (18) A single noble gas, typically Xenon, which is particularly ionizable, is used as fuel in a process that is over twice as efficient as traditional chemical thrusters. (18) Welcome to the world of ionized Propulsion.

Introduction to the Hall Effect Thruster

The Hall Effect Thruster is a type of electric propulsion, meaning it uses electric and magnetic fields to accelerate compressed gaseous Xenon propellant to incredible speeds creating thrust for the spacecraft. This differs from traditional chemical thrusters which burn liquid propellant to create thrust for the spacecraft.

Xenon Propellant

Xenon propellant is fuel composed of the noble gas, Xenon. The choice to use a noble gas as propellant is three-fold. First, Xenon is a large, heavy atom which produces high thrust. Second, it is easily ionized. Lastly, noble gasses are inert, meaning that if there is a fuel leak or malfunction within the propulsion system, there is no chance of a fire or explosion. (12   18)


    Benefits of Electric Propulsion

  • Improved efficiency:
    Hall Effect Thrusters have high specific impulse allowing for them to use significantly less propellant on a mission than with chemical thrusters. (12) For a general idea, an earlier form of electric propulsion, Ion thrusters, have demonstrated efficiencies as high as 90%. (18)

  • Increased lifetime:
    The Hall Effect thrusters used on the NASA PSYCHE mission have lifetimes of around 5,000 hours. To compare, space shuttle rocket boosters which use solid chemical propellant have operational times of only 2 minutes. (20)

  • Improved performance:
    Electric thrusters also have a lowered weight associated with their fuel. The Dawn spacecraft held only 937 lbs of Xenon propellant. (23) The space shuttle's orbiter holds roughly 11,000 lbs of liquid MMH fuel alone and a single space shuttle rocket booster has a solid propellant weight of 1,000,000 lbs. (20 24)

How do Hall Thrusters Work?


Anode and Cathode

A cathode (negatively charged electrode) at the downstream end of the chamber emits negatively charged electron particles. An anode (positively charged electrode) at the upstream end of the chamber injects neutral Xenon propellant.





Electromagnets

Powerful electromagnets on the outer edges and center of the chamber create a radial magnetic field. The 2 electrodes at each end of the chamber are responsible for the axial electric field.






The Electrons Travel

Some of the emitted electrons travel from the cathode to the chamber, and the forces from both the magnetic field and electric field trap the electrons at the downstream end of the chamber along a particular trajectory called an azimuthal drift.





The Xenon Travels

The Xenon propellant travels downstream toward the newly trapped electrons. Upon interaction with the electrons, the Xenon atoms become ionized, losing one of their electrons and converting them to positively charged Xenon ions. More specifically, when the neutral Xenon molecule comes into contact with electrons bound to the hall current (azimuthal drift), an electron in its outer 5p orbital becomes freed and travels to the anode in order to complete the discharge circuit.



Xenon

The electric field propels the Xenon ions out of the chamber at immense speeds. Note that if the thruster only propelled positive Xenon ions into space however, the spacecraft’s overall charge would become increasingly negative to compensate for the lost positive charges of Xenon. To resolve this, an equal number of electrons are exhausted along with the Xenon ions so that charge is conserved.
